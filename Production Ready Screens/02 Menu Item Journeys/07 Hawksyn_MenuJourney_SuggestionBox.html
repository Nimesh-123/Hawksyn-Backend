Two destinations

S07-B
Submit idea — category chips + text + submit
Submit
Design intent
This screen must feel like you are being heard — not filing a form. Opening prompt is invitational, not bureaucratic. "What would make Hawksyn more useful for you?" Category chips help the team route it fast.
Category chips
5 chips: Scan · HIP · Reports · Dashboard · Other. Tap one to select (amber fill). One selection max. Not mandatory — user can skip. Helps team triage without requiring a dropdown.

Idea echoed back
User's own text is reflected back in a quote card. This is the key wow moment — "they have it." Combined with the star icon and "Your idea is in." heading — it feels intentional, not automated.
Primary CTA
"See Community Requests →" is the primary CTA — pulls user into the board immediately while they are engaged. "Back to suggestions" is dim secondary — no button chrome.
S07-D
Community Requests — the living product board
Board
Board design
Three visual zones — Delivered (top, celebrated in green), WIP (middle, amber glow), Acknowledged (bottom, blue). Each card: idea text + category chip + status tag. "Yours" tag on the user's own submission. Scroll reveals more.
Visual hierarchy
Delivered = green card with tick icon. WIP = amber left border + subtle glow. Acknowledged = blue tag, neutral card. The further down you scroll, the earlier the stage. Delivered ideas feel like wins.

"Yours" tag
The user's own submission gets an amber "Yours" tag — they can immediately see their idea on the board alongside others. WIP status confirms it is being worked on. This closes the loop — idea → action → proof.
Section labels
Three coloured section dividers — green dot for Delivered, amber dot for WIP, blue dot for Acknowledged. Each has a faint coloured line extending across. Immediate visual scan of the whole board state.
S07-E
Delivered idea detail — someone's idea shipped — celebrate it
Delivered ✓
Why this exists
When a user taps a Delivered card, they see the full detail. If it was their own idea — a special message: "This was your idea. It shipped." If someone else's — "A community member suggested this. It shipped." Both feel good.
Product signal
Showing Delivered ideas proves the feedback loop is real — ideas actually become features. This is the most powerful retention and trust signal in the whole Suggestion Box experience.
